Your $100 on the MGUSD network

MoneyGram's U.S. dollar stablecoin on the Stellar blockchain — five partners working together to move one dollar anywhere.

MGUSD is issued by Bridge (a Stripe company), minted & burned by M0's smart contracts, custodied with Fireblocks, and recorded on Stellar.

You deposit $100 → 100 MGUSD appears in your in-app wallet

1

MoneyGram — your front door

You open the MoneyGram app (identity verification already done). You fund your balance — MoneyGram's network spans nearly 500,000 retail locations plus digital channels.

Plain EnglishLike depositing money at your local bank branch — except the "branch network" is one of the largest cash networks on Earth.

2

Bridge (a Stripe company) — the regulated issuer

Bridge is MGUSD's regulated, GENIUS Act-ready issuer. When a verified customer funds $100, Bridge authorizes the creation of 100 MGUSD. The GENIUS Act — federal stablecoin law signed July 18, 2025 — requires payment stablecoins to be backed 1:1 by dollars or other low-risk liquid assets, with monthly public reserve disclosures.

Plain EnglishThink of Bridge as the mint authority: a token only comes into existence when a real dollar stands behind it. No fractional magic.

3

M0 — the smart-contract factory

M0's smart-contract infrastructure executes the mint: 100 MGUSD are created on the Stellar blockchain — a permanent, tamper-evident record. M0 already powers $300M+ in on-chain supply across other stablecoin issuers.

Plain EnglishLike a vending machine that only dispenses tokens when the issuer inserts the coin. Automated code, not a person with a keyboard.

4

Fireblocks — the armored vault

MoneyGram holds MGUSD in institutional Fireblocks wallets, which are used to send tokens to your personal self-custodial wallet embedded in the MoneyGram app.

Plain EnglishLike an armored truck moving value from the mint to your pocket. You never touch raw blockchain plumbing.

5

Stellar — the public ledger

Every step is recorded on Stellar, a public blockchain built for payments: ledgers close in roughly 5 seconds and network fees run fractions of a cent. Stellar has been MoneyGram's blockchain partner since 2021.

Plain EnglishLike a courthouse record — anyone can verify it happened; no one can quietly rewrite it.

💵 You now hold 100 MGUSD in a self-custodial wallet — a dollar-denominated balance you can hold and access 24/7.

You redeem 100 MGUSD → you get $100 (or local currency)

1

You — tap withdraw in the app

You choose how to cash out through MoneyGram's network. Your 100 MGUSD leaves the self-custodial wallet in your app to be redeemed.

Plain EnglishLike writing a check — you start it, the system handles the rest.

2

Fireblocks — receives the tokens

Your MGUSD moves back into MoneyGram's institutional Fireblocks wallets — the same custody layer that distributed it — while redemption is processed.

Plain EnglishLike handing tokens to the cashier's window before collecting your cash. Secure and accountable.

3

M0 — burns the tokens

M0's smart-contract infrastructure burns (destroys) the redeemed MGUSD on Stellar. Total supply shrinks by exactly 100 — publicly visible on-chain.

Plain EnglishLike shredding a gift card after it's spent — the digital claim disappears so the real dollar can move again.

4

Bridge — settles the dollars

As the issuer, Bridge stands behind every token with reserves. Once tokens are burned, the corresponding dollars are settled back for payout.

Plain EnglishThe locked dollar is released — because the token that represented it no longer exists.

5

MoneyGram — pays you out

MoneyGram delivers your money through its global payments network — and you can convert into local currency when you need it, on your own terms.

Plain EnglishA family abroad can receive local currency; the sender funded dollars. The last mile is MoneyGram's home turf.

🌍 The full round trip: money in → digital dollars you control → money out through MoneyGram's global network.

Who does what

The five partners

Tap any card to see what each one is accountable for.

MoneyGram · Distribution

What are they accountable for?

The customer experience end to end. The app, identity verification, funding, and the final payout into your hands or your recipient's. MoneyGram serves over 60 million active customers through nearly 500,000 retail locations worldwide, with more than 70% of transactions now digital. They are the face of the ecosystem — and with MGUSD, the first global cash-payments network to issue its own dollar token on a public chain.

Bridge · Regulated issuer

What are they accountable for?

Issuance and regulatory integrity. Bridge — the stablecoin platform Stripe acquired for $1.1 billion in February 2025 — is MGUSD's "regulated, GENIUS Act-ready issuer." The GENIUS Act (signed July 18, 2025) is the first U.S. federal stablecoin law: it requires 100% reserve backing with dollars or other low-risk liquid assets and monthly public reserve disclosures, and restricts payment-stablecoin issuance to licensed issuers once its rules take effect. Keeping every MGUSD good for a dollar is Bridge's job.

M0 · Smart contracts

What are they accountable for?

The on-chain math. MGUSD tokens are minted and burned using M0's smart-contract infrastructure, deployed on Stellar at launch. When issuance is authorized, the contracts create exactly that amount — and burns destroy exactly what's redeemed, all publicly auditable. M0 is a modular stablecoin platform with more than $300 million in on-chain supply across other issuers.

Fireblocks · Custody

What are they accountable for?

Key security and custody. Digital tokens live in cryptographic wallets — lose the keys, lose the money. MoneyGram holds MGUSD in Fireblocks wallets, which are used to send tokens into individual customer self-custodial wallets embedded in the MoneyGram app. Fireblocks is the armored car and vault of the operation: institutional-grade wallet infrastructure between the mint and your pocket.

Stellar · Blockchain

What are they accountable for?

The immutable public record. Every mint, transfer, and burn is written permanently to Stellar — a blockchain purpose-built for payments, with ~5-second ledger closes and fees of fractions of a cent. MoneyGram and the Stellar Development Foundation have partnered since 2021 on stablecoin-powered money movement; MGUSD is, in their words, "the next chapter" of that five-year partnership.
