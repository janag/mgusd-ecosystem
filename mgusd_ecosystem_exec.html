What Is MGUSD

MGUSD is MoneyGram's native U.S. dollar stablecoin, launched June 2, 2026 on the Stellar blockchain — making MoneyGram the first global cash-payments network to issue its own dollar token on a public chain. It is integrated directly into the MoneyGram app as a self-custodial wallet, giving customers a stable, dollar-denominated balance they can hold and access 24/7, move globally, and convert into local currency when needed.

Core idea: A token only comes into existence when a real dollar stands behind it, and is destroyed ("burned") when that dollar is paid back out. Bridge (a Stripe company) is the regulated, GENIUS Act-ready issuer; M0's smart contracts mint and burn the tokens on Stellar; Fireblocks provides the wallet infrastructure; MoneyGram owns the customer relationship and the global cash network.

On-Ramp: You Deposit $100 → 100 MGUSD in Your App

Putting money into the network — each partner plays a distinct, sequential role.

On-Ramp Flow (Minting)
MoneyGram
Your front door — app, identity, funding
You open the MoneyGram app (identity verification already done) and fund your balance. MoneyGram's network spans nearly 500,000 retail locations plus digital channels.
Like depositing money at your local bank branch — except the "branch network" is one of the largest cash networks on Earth.
Bridge (Stripe)
The regulated issuer authorizes creation
Bridge is MGUSD's regulated, GENIUS Act-ready issuer. When a verified customer funds $100, Bridge authorizes the creation of 100 MGUSD. The GENIUS Act (signed July 18, 2025) requires payment stablecoins to be backed 1:1 by dollars or other low-risk liquid assets, with monthly public reserve disclosures.
Think of Bridge as the mint authority — a token only exists when a real dollar stands behind it. No fractional magic.
M0
Smart contracts execute the mint
M0's smart-contract infrastructure creates 100 MGUSD on the Stellar blockchain — a permanent, tamper-evident record. M0 already powers $300M+ in on-chain supply across other stablecoin issuers.
Like a vending machine that only dispenses tokens when the issuer inserts the coin — automated code, not a person with a keyboard.
Fireblocks
Custody delivers tokens to your wallet
MoneyGram holds MGUSD in institutional Fireblocks wallets, which are used to send tokens to your personal self-custodial wallet embedded in the MoneyGram app.
Like an armored truck moving value from the mint to your pocket — you never touch raw blockchain plumbing.
Stellar
The public ledger records everything
Every step is recorded on Stellar, a public blockchain built for payments: ledgers close in roughly 5 seconds and network fees run fractions of a cent. Stellar has been MoneyGram's blockchain partner since 2021.
Like a courthouse record — anyone can verify it happened; no one can quietly rewrite it.
Result: You now hold 100 MGUSD in a self-custodial wallet — a dollar-denominated balance you can hold and access 24/7, no banking hours, no wire cutoffs.

Off-Ramp: You Redeem 100 MGUSD → $100 (or Local Currency)

Taking money out reverses the flow — tokens are burned and dollars are paid out.

Off-Ramp Flow (Burning)
You / MoneyGram
You tap withdraw in the app
You choose how to cash out through MoneyGram's network. Your 100 MGUSD leaves the self-custodial wallet in your app to be redeemed.
Like writing a check — you start it, the system handles the rest.
Fireblocks
Custody receives the tokens
Your MGUSD moves back into MoneyGram's institutional Fireblocks wallets — the same custody layer that distributed it — while redemption is processed.
Like handing tokens to the cashier's window before collecting your cash.
M0
Smart contracts burn the tokens
M0's smart-contract infrastructure burns (destroys) the redeemed MGUSD on Stellar. Total supply shrinks by exactly 100 — publicly visible on-chain. The burn is a new ledger entry; history is never rewritten.
Like shredding a gift card after it's spent — the digital claim disappears so the real dollar can move again.
Bridge (Stripe)
The issuer settles the dollars
As the issuer, Bridge stands behind every token with reserves. Once tokens are burned, the corresponding dollars are settled back for payout.
The locked dollar is released — because the token that represented it no longer exists.
MoneyGram
Pays you out — anywhere
MoneyGram delivers your money through its global payments network — and you can convert into local currency when you need it, on your own terms.
A family abroad can receive local currency; the sender funded dollars. The last mile is MoneyGram's home turf.

The Five Partners & Their Accountability

Each partner owns a distinct failure domain — if something goes wrong, the responsible layer is clear.

MoneyGram Distribution

The customer experience end to end — the app, identity verification, funding, and the final payout into your hands or your recipient's. MoneyGram serves over 60 million active customers through nearly 500,000 retail locations worldwide, with more than 70% of transactions now digital. With MGUSD, it is the first global cash-payments network to issue its own dollar token on a public chain.

Bridge (a Stripe company) Regulated Issuer

Issuance and regulatory integrity. Bridge — the stablecoin platform Stripe acquired for $1.1 billion in February 2025 — is MGUSD's "regulated, GENIUS Act-ready issuer." The GENIUS Act (signed July 18, 2025) is the first U.S. federal stablecoin law: it requires 100% reserve backing with dollars or other low-risk liquid assets, monthly public reserve disclosures, and restricts payment-stablecoin issuance to licensed issuers once its rules take effect. Keeping every MGUSD good for a dollar is Bridge's job.

M0 Smart Contracts

The on-chain math. MGUSD tokens are minted and burned using M0's smart-contract infrastructure, deployed on Stellar at launch. When issuance is authorized, the contracts create exactly that amount — and burns destroy exactly what's redeemed, all publicly auditable. M0 is a modular stablecoin platform with more than $300 million in on-chain supply across other issuers.

Fireblocks Custody

Key security and custody. Digital tokens live in cryptographic wallets — lose the keys, lose the money. MoneyGram holds MGUSD in Fireblocks wallets, which are used to send tokens into individual customer self-custodial wallets embedded in the MoneyGram app. Fireblocks is the armored car and vault of the operation: institutional-grade wallet infrastructure between the mint and your pocket.

Stellar Blockchain

The immutable public record. Every mint, transfer, and burn is written permanently to Stellar — a blockchain purpose-built for payments, with ~5-second ledger closes and fees of fractions of a cent. MoneyGram and the Stellar Development Foundation have partnered since 2021 on stablecoin-powered money movement; MGUSD is, in their words, "the next chapter" of that five-year partnership.

What the Customer Sees vs. What's Underneath

The experience: a familiar banking-app feel — "invisible by design"

A customer just sees a dollar balance and activity in the MoneyGram app. But unlike a bank's private database, the balance is a real entry on the public Stellar ledger: the app is a window onto the chain, the wallet is self-custodial (the customer's keys control the funds), and anyone can verify supply and flows on a public Stellar explorer — addresses are pseudonymous, so identity stays private with MoneyGram's KYC records.

Key Takeaway

MGUSD is bookkeeping made public and programmable. Dollars enter through MoneyGram's cash network, become verifiable on-chain entries via Bridge and M0, move securely through Fireblocks custody, and exit as cash or local currency anywhere MoneyGram reaches. Each partner owns one accountable layer — distribution, issuance, contract execution, custody, and the ledger itself — so the system's trust doesn't rest on any single company's word.

Why it matters: The whole flow is auditable by anyone — supply, mints, and burns are public on Stellar in real time — while customer identity stays private with MoneyGram. That combination of radical transparency of activity and pseudonymity of identity is the structural advantage over traditional rails.
